What is an Open Product Developer

The all-new Mozilla Webmaker platform is looking for volunteer community members to help lead our non-profit, open source project in their local communities. We're looking for a small number of local volunteers to build the initial community, run community outreach and content creation events, be a role-model for participation on and off the platform, and work closely with the Webmaker team to provide feedback from the community to shape the product's future. In exchance, you'll get hands-on experience, training and support form Mozilla, and the chance to gain real-life experience launching and developing a consumer technology product.

The role

As an Open Product Developer, you will: ...

Responsibilities:

  • Build community. Build the Mozilla Webmaker community online and offline in your community
  • Organize events and workshops in your local community
  • Gather feedback and ideas from your local community on how to make the product better.
  • Teach others how to use Webmaker, create great content, and increase their digial skills.
  • Be a role model for youth and embody the Mozilla values of service, openness and digital opportunity
  • Moderate content and monitor behaviour
  • Make the prodcut better. File bugs, do local user testing and communicate with the Mozilla Webmaker development team
  • Communicate with your community through social media, at in-person events and through local media.

Suggested time commitment:

  • ...

We're looking for a willingness to make a 6 month commitment to the role, understanding that this is the first time we're trying it and may need to adjust.

Qualifications:

You're an ideal Open Product Developer if you have...

  • Experience building and leading community
  • Demonstrated positive participation in existing communities
  • Familiarity with online collaborative development tools (e.g., Transifex, GitHub, IRC, Skype)
  • Familiarity with social media and messaging platforms (e.g., Facebook/Twitter/YouTube/Snapchat/WhatsApp)

Support and Recognition from Mozilla

  • Experience. Gain real-life experience launching and developing a consumer technology product.
  • Training. Be paired with a mentor from Mozilla Foundation
  • Leadership experience. And professional skills development
  • Put Mozilla on your resume. At the end of your successfuly volunteer experience, you will receive a letter of recommendation from a Mozilla staff member.
  • Mozilla merchandise.

How to apply

Fill out an Open Product Developer questionaire.

Also, a good way to tell us more about yourself is to publish a blog post or a thread in Discourse that tells us how and why you've had a hand in community, product, and volunteering in the past.
